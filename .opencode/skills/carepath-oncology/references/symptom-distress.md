# Psychological Distress (Anxiety, Depression, Sleep) - Symptom Management Pathway

## Objective
Screen, identify, and manage psychological distress, depression, anxiety, and sleep disturbances in cancer patients. Distress is a "sixth vital sign" in oncology and requires proactive management.

## Duration
**Ongoing** throughout treatment and survivorship

### 4. C-SSRS (Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale) - **CRITICAL**
- **Screening Questions**:
  1. Have you wished you were dead?
  2. Have you had thoughts of killing yourself?
  3. Have you thought about how you might do this? (Method)
  4. Have you had some intention of acting on thoughts? (Intent)
  5. Have you started to work out or worked out the details of how to kill yourself? (Plan)

- **Action**:
  1. **Same-day Psychiatry/Psychology Consult**.
  2. Establish **Safety Plan** (remove means, identify support).

  2. Follow-up within 48 hours.
  3. Provide crisis hotline numbers (988).

## Management Strategy

### 1. Psychological Distress (General)
- **Mild (Distress <4, PHQ/GAD <10)**:
  - Psychoeducation, peer support groups.
  - Relaxation techniques (breathing, meditation).
- **Moderate/Severe (Distress â‰¥4, PHQ/GAD â‰¥10)**:
  - **Psychotherapy**: CBT, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT).
  - **Pharmacotherapy**: SSRIs (Escitalopram, Sertraline), SNRIs (Venlafaxine - also helps hot flashes/neuropathy).
  - **Referral**: Psycho-oncology, Psychiatry, Social Work.

### 2. Sleep Management
- **Sleep Hygiene (First-Line)**:
  - Consistent wake time.
  - Bed is for sleep and sex ONLY (stimulus control).
  - Limit caffeine/screens.
- **Treat Underlying Causes**:
  - Pain (analgesics), Anxiety (anxiolytics), Steroids (take before noon).
- **Pharmacologic**:
  - Melatonin (3-5mg).
  - Trazodone (25-50mg).
  - **Avoid Benzodiazepines** for chronic sleep issues (fall risk, delirium).

## Monitoring & Follow-Up

### Daily/Weekly Patient Tasks
- **Mood Log**: Rate mood/anxiety daily.
- **Sleep Log**: Bedtime, wake time, interruptions.

### Clinical Team Review
- **Weekly**: Review PHQ-9/GAD-7 scores if elevated.
- **Every Visit**: Distress Thermometer screening.

## Comorbidity Considerations

### Depression + Pain
- **Bidirectional**: Pain worsens depression; depression lowers pain threshold.
- **Action**: Treat BOTH simultaneously. SNRIs (Duloxetine, Venlafaxine) treat both pain and depression.

### Anxiety + Dyspnea
- **Cycle**: Anxiety causes shortness of breath â†’ panic â†’ worse dyspnea.
- **Action**: Treat underlying respiratory cause + consider low-dose benzodiazepine (Lorazepam) or SSRI for anxiety component.

### Delirium vs. Depression
- **Hypoactive Delirium**: Can mimic depression (withdrawn, quiet).
- **Differentiation**: Delirium has **fluctuating consciousness** and **inattention**.
- **Action**: If confused/disoriented, evaluate for medical causes (infection, meds, metabolic) - DO NOT just start antidepressant.

---

## Patient Education
- **"It's not just in your head"**: Distress is a chemical and physiological response to cancer.
- **Safety Planning**: Keep crisis numbers saved in phone.
- **Medication Myths**: Antidepressants are not "happy pills" and are not addictive; they restore chemical balance.
